STRAWBERRY CUSTARD RECIPE

Ingredients This simple recipe uses common ingredients and relies on either a pre-made custard powder or a scratch-made base. Using Custard Powder (Quick Method):
2 cups whole milk
2–3 tbsp strawberry or vanilla custard powder
½ cup sugar (adjust to taste)
1 cup fresh strawberries, finely chopped
From Scratch (Richer Method):
2 cups whole milk
1½ cups strained strawberry puree (from about 5 cups fresh strawberries)
4 egg yolks
½ cup granulated sugar
3 tbsp cornstarch
3 tbsp cubed butter (optional, for richness)
1 tsp vanilla extract (if not using vanilla bean)

Instructions


Quick Method (Using Custard Powder)


Prepare the Slurry: In a small bowl, take a few tablespoons of cold milk and whisk in the custard powder until you have a smooth, lump-free paste.
Heat the Milk: In a saucepan, heat the remaining milk and the sugar over medium heat until it comes to a gentle boil, stirring to dissolve the sugar.
Thicken the Custard: Reduce the heat to low and slowly pour the custard slurry into the boiling milk while whisking continuously to prevent lumps.
Cook: Continue cooking and stirring for about 2–3 minutes until the custard thickens to a creamy consistency.
Cool and Add Fruit : Remove the pan from the heat and allow the custard to cool completely to room temperature. Stir occasionally while cooling to prevent a skin from forming. Once cooled, mix in the chopped fresh strawberries.
Chill: Pour the mixture into serving bowls and refrigerate for at least 1-2 hours (or until fully chilled) before serving.
